Briyani

In South Asian cooking, one of the most famous rice dishes is biryani, which is aromatic and savory. Cooked rice is usually layered with spicy meat, veggies, or both to make the dish. The rich and complex flavor of biryani is attributed to the combination of spices used in the meal. Biryani comes in a wide variety of regional varieties, each having a distinct flavor and preparation technique. Popular throughout South Asia and beyond, biryani is frequently served during festivities and special occasions. People all throughout the world continue to enjoy this pleasant and delectable dinner.

Methods

  • Wash the rice thoroughly in cold water and soak it in water for at least 30 minutes.
  • Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  • In a large pot, heat the oil or ghee on medium heat.
  • Add the sliced onions and sauté until they are golden brown.
  • Add the ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, vegetables (if using), and biryani masala, and sauté for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.
  • Add the chicken or lamb pieces to the pot and sauté until they are browned.
  • In a separate pot, bring the 2-3 cups of water or chicken broth to a boil. Add the rice and cook until it is about 80% done. Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  • Mix the yogurt and salt into the pot with the chicken or lamb and stir well.
  • Layer the rice on top of the chicken or lamb mixture in the pot. Sprinkle saffron or yellow food color on top of the rice (if using).
  • Cover the pot and cook on low heat for 20-30 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy.
  • Garnish with chopped cilantro and mint leaves before serving. Biryani can be served with raita, pickle, or any other side dish of your choice.
